Cover One

Cover One, also known as Man Free, is a defensive scheme where one safety is dropped back into coverage while every other defender plays man coverage.

There is usually an extra defender remaining, who can either blitz or drop back to provide extra protection against slants and other short crossing routes.

Cover Two

Cover Two is more of a "zone" defense: a defense where players are assigned to an area of the field to cover. The safeties will be positioned just outside the hashes about 15 yards back from the line of scrimmage, and they each monitor their area from the middle of the field to their respective sideline.

The linebackers are tasked with monitoring the part of the field 5-12 yards back from the line of scrimmage in a formation called Tampa 2. They cover three zones among the three of them.

Cover Three

Cover Three is a zone defense which aims to solve the middle-of-the-field vulnerability that Cover 2 presents. One safety drops back to guard the middle about 15 yards off the line of scrimmage, while this time, the corners play off man coverage and sink back about 12 yards off the line of scrimmage. They guard the sidelines, but if a receiver runs a streak route and passes them, the corners switch to man coverage and follow that receiver.

The linebackers and strong safety, meanwhile, split the field horizontally into four parts, with each defender being assigned a part to cover. This makes it hard for offenses to run short completion routes over the middle.

Cover Four

Cover Four is a zone defense that completely ruins a pass-happy offense's day. With four deep defenders (two corners on the oustide and two safeties within the hashes) and three linebackers underneath, Cover Four aims to eliminate the deep pass.

This defense is growing increasingly common in the NFL to counter the increase of pass-attack offenses. You'll also find this defense common during last-minute miracle-drive situations for the offense since the last thing the defense wants to do in that moment is to give up a long-bomb touchdown.
